THE IRON MATRIARCH (1980s)

After losing her husband when her daughter Amina was just nine years old, Sarah Hersi Ali became the sole provider for six children.

Most would have retreated. She expanded. She pulled her daughters into the business young. She was their first business school. She taught them that a handshake is a contract, that inventory is cash, and that resilience is the only currency that never devalues.

"She was both mother and father to us. The shop floor was our classroom."
